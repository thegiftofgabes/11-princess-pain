A PREMISE

This game assumes an explicitly queer and anti-capitalist take on the Urban Fantasy genre. You don’t need to be queer to enjoy it, but i don’t think capitalists are gonna have much fun here.

No matter what they tell you, there’s still weirdness and wonder everywhere. You just have to know where to look. At the edges and cracks of ‘normal’ life we exist, we persist, and we resist: the monsters, the magicians, the anomalies, the freaks, and the outcasts. We gather in the shadows, trying our best to live our lives in a world that, when it doesn’t exactly fear or hate us, doesn’t even believe in our existence.

Yes, I know this can be a metaphor for queer experience, but it’s not just that. This is about the monstrous, the abnormal, and the weird, in any flavour you want, not as something to be feared but to be cherished and protected.

Alas, there are people out there who can’t stand this. They work from behind the scenes to protect ‘normality’ at all costs. This means getting rid of us, by any means necessary. They are often successful, but not as much as they’d like. Even so, they are resourceful, and they’re good at what they do.

There are also those who would use the magical and the unusual for their own purposes, be it profit, knowledge or power (which, one could argue, are the same thing in the end). These are even more dangerous when they manage to get what they want.

But we’re still here. We’re not going anywhere. And we fight back.

A BEGINNING

You are all members of an organisation dedicated to protecting the weird, the monstrous, the anomalous, and the magical. You are also, in some way or another, weird, monstrous, abnormal, anomalous, or magical yourselves. Preferably for me, you’re a bunch of anarcho-communist queers as well, but you do you, no judgement. Just know that no bigots of any kind are allowed to play this game, at the risk of being hexed, cursed and otherwise jinxed. Remember: no bigots, only bigfoots.

Your group is not all-powerful. It is not associated with any government or corporation. It is definitely not structured in a militaristic or hierarchical model. It does not have stuff like a ‘budget’ or ‘orders from the higher-ups’. It is, however, organised and coordinated; it has to be. It was created with some goals in mind: protect others like you from persecution and exploitation by people like The Bureau or The Brotherhood (more on those later), prevent stupid assholes (like nazis) from using magic to achieve their nefarious goals, and overall, keep the world weird.

This game is, in a way, a response to a lot of things. Most of all, Hellboy (and the BPRD) and the SCP Foundation, but also books by Neil Gaiman (particularly American Gods, Anansi Boys, Neverwhere and The Books of Magic), Charles Stross (The Laundry Files series) and China Miéville (specifically Kraken), and games like Delta Green or the World of Darkness universe. Some of it was taken from a forgotten Dresden Files RPG campaign I planned many years ago and some of it came from the notes for a novel I’m writing as a middle finger to some transphobic fantasy writers. It’s also influenced by getting annoyed at how many nazis there are in occult circles. Overall, it’s my take on the Urban Fantasy genre and its tropes, while feeling monstrous myself. It’s taking bits and pieces from all those different things and stitching together a monster in my own fractured image.

This is a game about monsters, anomalies, magicians and other weirdos fighting back and protecting the oppressed against those who would exploit and erase them. And punching nazis, of course, for all nazis must be punched.

Ok, moving on.

Before starting the game, discuss the need for safety tools, which are tools that facilitate the creation of a comfortable and safe environment for all of you. I like the Lines and Veils system and the X-Card, but choose whatever suits you best. Most of all, be kind and trust each other.

Next step is creating a manifesto together: take turns declaring an element, any element, you’d like to either have or not have in your game. For example, I want meat witches, you don’t want cellphones, she wants tentacles or they don’t want capitalism. In any case of disagreements, state your arguments and have a vote. Or flip a coin. Whatever works for you. The list is finished when someone says something like “ok, I think we have enough already.”

Finally, decide which one of you will play as the person formerly-known-as-GM / DM / Referee / whatever, who mediates conflicts, calls for rolls, narrates the adventures and plays the NPCs. Come up with a title for them. I’ll call them the Organizer for now.

YOUR CHARACTER

Come up with a name and/or a callsign for your character, what the others can call them, and the pronouns they use.

Think of a background for your character: what did they do before this story began? Alternatively, you can also roll 1d100 on a list of premade backgrounds below.

Describe what your character looks like. Ask another player to give a small detail they think is fitting for your character. Use it if you like it, or twist it in your own image if you don’t.

Assign two tags for each of the following verbs:

  • BE: anything that significantly defines your character. (e.g., ‘deeply empathetic’, ‘amateur coroner’, ‘none more goth’, ‘life of the party’ etc.)

  • HAVE: anything important that your character carries with them, literally or not. (e.g., ‘my mother’s sword’, ‘a worn grimoire’, ‘a broken heart’, ‘the hopes of my people’ etc.)

  • DO: anything your character knows how to do especially well. (e.g., ‘car theft’, ‘kung fu fighting’, ‘talking myself out of trouble’, ‘eating literally anything’ etc.)

Tags are not rigid. You can HAVE ‘friends in high places’, or you can BE ‘friends with influential people’, for example.

If you want your character to use Magic, you need a MAGIC TAG. That means one of your tags (BE, HAVE or DO, you choose) should explicitly state that you can use Magic in some way. More on how magic works in the ‘Our Game’ section.

OPTIONAL MECHANICS

If you’re playing a campaign or longer game, you can use the following guidelines for advancing character arcs: define two things your character WANTS and one thing they NEED.

Their WANTS represent their conscious objectives. Their NEED represents something that they have to go through, but not necessarily something they want or consciously know about, in order to grow and give their story a satisfying ‘ending’. These are not fixed and can change as you play and as the story leads you to places you didn’t anticipate.

  • Examples of wants: avenge the death of my parents; become the master of my craft; find a cure for my affliction; learn the secrets of the old world etc.

  • Examples of needs: overcome a trauma; learn to care for the smaller things in life; accept myself; find forgiveness etc.

If your character gets what they need before getting what they want, they can either retire in peace or receive a revelation that will make all rolls related to their wants easier (explained below).

If your character gets both their wants without getting what they need, their wants will turn out to be not as satisfying as imagined and you can either give up and retire your character or make them go through their darkest hour (see below) to receive a revelation that will make all rolls related to their need easier (explained below).

Whenever you want (or need), define your character’s darkest hour. This is the lowest point in their story, the inevitable moment where they have to face their deepest fears. You can choose to play it out or just narrate it, whatever works for you.

Both WANTS and NEEDS have Beats. Beats are steps your character has to take in order to get what they want and what they need. Beats are not necessarily the same as successes or achievements, they are narratively important moments, and can even include failures and defeats. When playing, assign 4 beats to each of your wants and your need. Every time you reach a beat you can change all the other remaining beats and one of your tags for something else, if you can justify how that makes sense in the story.

You don’t need to start with any of your beats already written.

When you reach a beat, cross it out. Each beat crossed makes the rolls related to reaching the next beat in the list harder, in the following order:

  • After BEAT 1: roll 3d6, take the two lowest results.

  • After BEAT 2: roll 2d6, minus 1.

  • After BEAT 3: roll 2d6, minus 2.

Each beat crossed also makes easier the rolls related to their respective wants or need that could end in dying or quitting: roll 2d6 + 1d6 for each beat, take the two highest values.

The wants and needs system is intended to provide you with open-ended long-term goals, but if it still feels too restrictive for you, here’s an alternative:

  • Don’t worry about general wants and needs. Instead, come up with 2 short-term beats for things your character wants, and 1 for what you think they need. When you reach a beat, cross it off and come up with the next one. Everything else works the same.

After you create your characters, describe how they met, how and when did they join the group, and what unites them beyond their missions.

CHARACTER BACKGROUNDS

The suggested backgrounds give you ideas for your tags and if/why you can use Magic. A few of them also include custom mechanics. Those are just suggestions, do what you want with them.

If you’re only interested in the more regular human character backgrounds, roll 1d20. If you want to open it up a little for more supernatural options, roll 2d20 and add them together. If you want all the options, roll 1d100.

1. Antiquarian

Somebody call Marie Kondo, we’ve found a hoarder! Can all that junk really spark joy? Take one (magical?) item that “sparks joy” as a HAVE tag, explain why you’re in this line of work with a BE tag, and define your speciality within the field with a DO tag.

2. Artist

You’re desperately in need of some inspiration. What unconventional avenues have you pursued in search of it? Define your craft with a DO or BE tag and take some appropriate tools as a HAVE tag.

3. Bartender

What’s the weirdest rumour you’ve heard whispered by shadowy figures near closing time? In your line of work, you gotta be a good listener. Take that and plus your people skills as BE or DO tags. Take something alcoholic as a HAVE - you’re gonna need it, even if it’s not for you.

4. Clown

Oh, great Pagliacci! Does life seem harsh and cruel? Do you feel all alone in a threatening world where what lies ahead is vague and uncertain? Give yourself some tragicomic BE tag; a marvellous circus skill as a DO tag, and some props as a HAVE tag.

5. Conspiracy Theorist

You know The Truth about the World. And They probably know you do. And You know They ‘know’ it. And– Anyway, what is this Truth, again? And who are ‘They’? You must have acquired some ‘specialised’ knowledge, contacts, and items in your unwavering search for What’s Really Going On. Write those as tags.

6. Construction Worker

What sort of oddity did you find hidden inside a wall? How did that lead to hanging out with all these weird people (and not-people)? Take some muscles as a BE or DO tag and your work tools as a HAVE.

7. Cook

What was the most peculiar dish you have ever been asked to cook? What’s the otwherworldly flavour you long to taste one day? Take a sharp kitchen knife as a HAVE and how to use it as a DO.

8. Digital Influencer

You’ll do anything for those likes. And having enough of them will allow you to do almost anything. How do you wield this kind of power? Take a smartphone and your followers as valuable HAVE tags and represent your personal appeal with either a BE or a DO tag.

9. Doctor

How did you end up here, Doc? Do you know how to care for nonhuman bodies? Do you know how to use anomalous methods to treat your patients? And if so, are you willing to? How valuable are your medical skills for the community? Define your medical speciality with a DO tag and take a first aid kit as a HAVE.

10. Drag Queen

You better lip-sync for your life, girl. Your makeup is your armour. Onstage you’re unstoppable. But what about your life offstage? Give yourself a couple of shiny BE or DO tags that showcase your performance skills and add your stage paraphernalia as a HAVE.

11. Filmmaker

What kind of films do you make? Are you an amateur or are you already (in)famous? Have you ever caught the supernatural on-camera? Take a camera as a HAVE tag, and a snobbish knowledge about bizarre European directors or an encyclopaedic understanding of genre tropes (or both) as a DO.

12. Hacker

You know what those green characters on black screens mean in that movie. Not that one, the other one. There are a lot of peculiar things to learn online if you know where to look… and as it happens, you actually do. Represent your arcane knowledge with a tag. Now get up from that chair, stretch a little, and take your favourite gadget as a HAVE tag and a hacking-related BE or DO tag.

13. Journalist

The public has a right to know, and you will do whatever it takes to uncover the Truth-with-capital-T. Do you work for a major conglomerate or maybe an independent blog? What’s your usual beat? Crime, culture, politics? Write your trusted sources and hidden recorder as HAVE tags and your journalistic skills as DO tags.

14. Mechanic

You’re great with machines, engines, and all sorts of contraptions. How did you learn your craft? Can you fix anomalous mechanisms? Grab your toolkit as a HAVE tag and your mechanical skills as a DO.

15. Musician

Where was the most unusual place you’ve ever had to perform in? What genres and instruments do you usually play? Incidentally, are you any good? Take your instrument(s) as a HAVE tag and your performance skills as a BE or DO. Maybe grab a copy of Marvelous Mutations & Merry Musicians too, it’s up to you.

16. Private Investigator

What seemingly unsolvable case keeps you up at night? What, or who, are you searching for now? What did you see in the dark of a dirty back alley? Take a gun and either a camera or a flask of whisky as HAVE tags.

17. Retired Football Player

The parties never stop, but you’re still bored. Where has your ennui taken you? Are you still rich and famous, or have you already burned through your fortune? Give yourself a divorce, a couple of nice thighs, and great athletic skills.

18. Secret Agent

You used to work as a spy for The Man, but something made you leave. What was it? Nevertheless, you’re still great at what you do and even managed to keep a few fancy gadgets and trusted contacts. Write your tags accordingly.

19. Teacher

Your job is to share and nurture knowledge with those who need it. Do you enjoy it? Why do you do it? Do your students like you? Write your expertise as a BE or DO tag, and take a well-stocked collection about it as a HAVE.

20. Writer

You swear you’re working on that novel. What do you usually write? Poetry, short stories, nonfiction? Take a mostly useless degree, procrastination, and a lot of coffee. Write your abilities with words as a DO or BE tag, and a journal full of notes and random facts as a HAVE.

21. Alchemist

Not really ‘just Chemistry’, not really ‘just Magic’, but something else in its own right. How do you perceive your craft? What made you pursue it? Take some bizarre reagents and your favourite creation as HAVE tags. You can take a MAGIC TAG if you want, but call it Alchemy.

22. Alien Abductee

One day you were taken. Afterwards, nobody believed you. How has it affected your body and mind? Can you remember anything? What have they done to you? What did they look like? Give yourself a confusing body modification and a few good ufologist friends.

23. Chosen-by-an-Elder-God

You were chosen, but have no idea why. There must be a reason, right? Sure, the tentacles may be a little too much, but they’re handy. And you’re already used to Its whispers in your ears. What does It tell you? Take a boon from It as a HAVE tag and a related uncanny ability as a DO. You can take a MAGIC TAG if you want, but call it prayer.

24. Escaped Human Sacrifice

The stars were right. Everything was going according to plan. Until you managed to break out of your bonds right before the obsidian dagger could pierce your heart — or something along those lines — and in doing so you ruined a ritual decades in the making. Why were you chosen? What did you take with you before escaping? Are they still after you? Write your tags accordingly.

25. Ex-cultist

Those people you used to run with were surely something, huh? What was it like for you, back then? What did you do together? Why did you leave? Do you miss them? Do you fear them? Grab an ancient artefact you took on your way out as a HAVE tag. You can take a MAGIC TAG if that’s your thing.

26. Family Curse Inheritor

Your ancestors left you a pretty shitty inheritance, going back generations. What is it? Do you all turn into frogs when kissed? Or is it a haunted house? A creepy music box? Please tell me it’s not a creepy music box. How long has your family had to deal with it? Have you been prepared to face it? Take a tag that represents your curse and another for whatever family you still have left.

27. Firestarter

This. Girl. Is on fireee! You can generate and/or control flames with your mind. How do you do it? Is it hard? Do you have complete control over it? What happens if you lose control? Can you get burned? When did your powers first manifest? Treat your powers as if you were using Magic, but could only use the Magic Word FIRE!

28. Magician

Not a Witch. Not a Shaman. Definitely not an Occultist. Still, you know how to use Magic. But how? Magic is not some super-power that you’re just born with. So, why can you do it? Did you learn it through ancient books? Did someone teach it to you, or what? And how do you do it? Chanting some words, waving your hands, brandishing a wand, spilling some blood? Describe that with your MAGIC TAG.

29. Medium

You love some gossip, and the dead surely deliver. Do they only appear when you call or do they randomly intrude into your daily life? What was the scariest thing you’ve seen behind the veil? Do you have regular visitors? Whenever you contact the dead, roll a d6. If the result is a 1, they are pissed. If it’s between 2 and 4, you can speak to the spirits, but something else notices you too. On a 5 or 6, you’re safe. If I were you, I’d take some kind of amulet to ward off evil spirits as a HAVE tag too.

30. Occultist

Oh, so you do Real Magick, huh? The key is the k, right? Fine, you can take a MAGIC TAG if you want. But please try to keep the details of your solo Sex Magick rituals to yourself. So… what’s your thing? Thelema, Chaos Magick, diabolism, or something else? Take the appropriate paraphernalia as HAVE tags.

31. Oracle

You can tell the future, but only in pretty vague terms. How do your prophecies appear to you? Do they come as dreams, visions, whispers in your ear? Do you need to get high to see them? Or maybe it’s only when you dream? Whenever you meet that trigger, roll a d6. If the result is between 1 and 3, you can’t remember the vision afterwards. If it’s 4 or 5, you can recall it as mysterious metaphors to be deciphered. If you get a 6, their meaning is clear and you immediately understand them.

32. Possessed

There’s someone or something else inside you. Sometimes you can keep it in check. Sometimes you lose control. Sometimes it takes over your body. Where do you go when it’s in charge? How well do you two get along? How green is your puke? Ask someone else to write half your tags. You can use those if you allow whatever’s possessing you to take over for a moment. They can take a MAGIC TAG if they want — you probably can’t.

33. Preacher

Repent heathens, for you have arrived! You’re a true believer, and no one can deny your faith. What do you believe in? You can take a MAGIC TAG if you want, but treat it as prayer. Take a sacred relic as a HAVE tag. If you want some inspiration, feel free to have a look at the tables in Apotheosis Janitors.

34. Reanimator

You can raise the dead. But how do you do it? Mad science? Necromancy? Something else? And why would you do that, anyway? What do you do with those you re-animate? Are they just shambling zombies or do they have consciousness? Where do you get the ‘supplies’? You can take a MAGIC TAG or the appropriate tools and skills, accordingly.

35. Shaman

You’re supposed to be wise. Are you? You are the keeper of the knowledge of your ancestors. You have access to the spirit world and beyond by entering a trance. You’re a healer, a guide, a seer. Do you feel up to the task? Do you get into a trance through song and dance or other methods? Take a MAGIC TAG, and a HAVE tag related to your practice.

36. Telepath

Peeking inside people’s thoughts is actually not as interesting as you might think. It takes a lot of effort to get through the noise and the nonsense. When have you started having this ability? How much control do you have over it? Do you have any power over the thoughts you access? Treat your powers as if you were using Magic, but could only use the Magic Word MIND.

37. Underworld Explorer

There are many places below baseline reality, both literally and figuratively. You explore them, for whatever reason. But no matter how much you descend, you feel you gotta dig ever deeper. Is it an obsession, a job, a hobby, all of these? How has it changed you? Take the appropriate equipment and one weird finding as HAVE tags.

38. Winged Person

You… have wings, I guess? How many? What are they made of? Feathers? Leather? Metal? Your own skin, grotesquely stretched over your hollow bones? Were you born with them, or were they given to you, willingly or not? How do you hide them if needed? If they allow you to fly, write that as a DO tag.

39. Witch

Everything is a part of nature, you just happen to be more attuned to your place in it, and the powers you can channel from there. Are you a wise elder that knows all about herbs? A charming youth that consorts with devils by the moonlight? Maybe both, depending on your mood? How many warts do you have? Take a MAGIC TAG, a familiar, a broom, whatever, you know, witch-stuff. Just don’t start with gender essentialist bullshit or saying you’re the daughter of the witches they couldn’t burn, else you’ll be cursed to play the most boring game of here, there, be monsters! ever.

40. Witness

You saw things you shouldn’t, and now you can’t unsee them. In a moment, all of your assumptions were shattered and you found yourself in a world full of horror and magic. You need to know more and more about it, and it’s starting to look like an addiction. What happened? How has that changed you? You can take a MAGIC TAG if you want, and a few precious connections on the anomalous underworld as a HAVE.

41. Alien

You’re not from around here, but that doesn’t mean you don’t belong. Where are you from? What do you look like? How did you get here? What’s your attitude towards the Earth? Are there others like you? Do you feel alone? You can take a MAGIC TAG if you want and whatever alien tech or skills you’ve got as HAVE or DO tags.

42. Anthropomorphic Manifestation

You’re the physical embodiment of something. What is it? You have enormous power over this domain, but only this. How did you come here? Have you existed as long as the thing you manifest as? Or are you a recent arrival? Were you summoned to this plane? Have you spontaneously appeared? You may take a MAGIC TAG if you want, but you can only use Magic related to the thing you manifest.

43. A bunch of Goblins in a Beekeeper suit

Nobody took you seriously before, but now they see you eye-to-eye. Well, one of you, at least – whoever is the Head today. How many of you are there? And why bees? Take that suit and a smoke machine as HAVE tags and beekeeping as a DO tag.

44. Atlantis Refugee

Your home is forever gone, sunken beneath the waves. How did you survive the cataclysm? Do you know what caused it? What did you manage to take with you? Who gave you shelter after the Fall? Are there other survivors? How do you resemble modern humans, and how do you differ? Did you have supernatural abilities? What about super-advanced tech? You can take an ancient Atlantean relic as a HAVE and a MAGIC TAG, if you want one.

45. Automaton

You want to make it clear that you are not a ‘robot’. Those are just mindless machines, while your delicate clockwork circuits are actually capable of independent thought. Do you remember who built you and with what purpose? How and when did you get free? Are you humanoid in appearance, or something more uncanny? Can you have feelings and sensations? Write your tags accordingly.

46. Bigfoot

You’re not very photogenic, but that’s alright. People keep trying to catch you on-camera, but you just wanna be left alone. Why is that? Do you feel lonely? Are you the last of your species? Are you a missing link? Are you just very large and hairy? How big are your feet, by the way?

47. Boto

You’re a bright pink river dolphin that can turn into a remarkably hot person at will, except for the blowhole on your forehead. Rumour has it that you just do that to have sex with humans and get them pregnant. I mean, not judging if you have a breeding kink or anything, but, seriously, do you really do that? Take a MAGIC TAG if you want and write your underwater-breathing abilities as a DO or BE tag and a HAVE tag that hides your hole.

48. Brain-In-A-Jar

You’re a brain. In a jar. Submerged in a stinky fluid, full of wires and other hi-tech equipment. So how the hell did you get there? Did a flying fungus-crustacean thing from Pluto try to steal your brain? Or was it a nazi mad scientist? A horrible lab accident? How do you manage to interact with the world now? Do you have a prosthetic body you can sometimes inhabit? Write your tags accordingly.

49. Caipora

Tiny, with dark skin completely covered in red hair, you used to roam the forest, usually naked and riding a giant boar. Your duty was to protect the wild animals from hunters. How exactly you achieved that is up to debate. Some say you ‘just’ made them get lost in the forest, using your backwards-facing feet to create false tracks. Others say you hunted them and ate their flesh. You can also resurrect dead animals if needed, but you prefer not to let them die in the first place. People say you smoke like a chimney, but are terrified of fire. What’s your truth? What made you leave your beloved forest? Take a MAGIC TAG and your cigarettes and Giant Boar as HAVE tags.

50. Catperson

UwU meow you’re probably trans or something. Also, you look mostly like an unanomalous human with some feline traits. How many and which ones do you have? Pointy ears? A fluffy tail? Vertical pupils? Fangs? Depending on what traits you have, you will also have corresponding abilities, such as but not limited to: pouncing, jumping, seeing in the dark, heightened senses, commanding an army of otakus etc. Write your tags accordingly.

51. Centaur

our game

Do whatever you want and deal with the consequences.

If you try to do something with potentially dangerous or uncertain outcomes, or if the stakes are just too high, roll 2d6 and add them. If any of your tags could help you in this action, roll 3d6 and use the two highest values.

  • A result of 6 or less is either a failure or a success with disastrous or completely unexpected consequences, which the Organizer decides.
  • A total of 7 to 9 is a “ok, but…” narrated by the Organizer.
  • 10 or more means you did good and get to tell us how you did it.

Combat is not special. Just do the same stuff as the other actions.

Conditions

Whenever you fuck up, get hurt, or something happens to you that has potentially lasting consequences, take a condition tag. Some examples of conditions include: a lasting headache, a broken arm, nausea, a really loud & squeaky shoe, a horrible smell, an acid trip - things like that.

When making a roll, if a condition could get in your way, roll 3d6 and use the lowest values. If any of your tags could help you out in this situation, they cancel each other out and you just roll 2d6.

You can have a maximum of 3 conditions at once. You can get rid of them by taking narratively appropriate actions (as agreed by the group) or after you get to rest and enough time has passed.

When you already have 3 conditions, the next time you should take one, roll a d6. A 5 or 6 means you get to narrate how you miraculously escape it. 1 to 4 means either this condition or one of the previous 3 becomes permanent and you’ll have to swap one of your tags for it, with an appropriately dramatic scene. The next time another condition becomes permanent, your character either dies, gives up or, if using the optional wants and needs rules, swaps one of their wants to get rid of this condition or one of their needs to learn to live with it.

Magic

If you are a Magic user, give yourself a tag that says you can use Magic, your MAGIC TAG. In return, write down 1d4 Magic Words from the d66 table below, representing spells that you can easily use.

If you cast a spell that uses one of those words in any significant way, roll a d6 for each word used and keep the highest. This die is the measure of how well you did:

  • A 6 means an awesome success and you get an extra positive effect.
  • A 4 or 5 means you get exactly what you wanted.
  • A 2 or 3 means you were just ok and the spell has a lesser effect than you expected.
  • On a 1, it goes horribly wrong and backfires.

Be creative about how you use your Magic Words. Puns are not only allowed, but encouraged, Magic likes wordplay for some reason.

Some specific backgrounds will suggest alternative ways of using the Magic Words. For example, the Pyromancer’s powers are like using Magic but only with the Magic Word FIRE. Telepaths only have the Magic Words MIND or EMOTION. This is a shortcut for representing other supernatural powers that are not exactly Magic while still allowing for failure and cleverness. You can use those as examples for your own uncanny abilities. As with everything else in this book, you don’t have to follow those rules.

Beyond the Magic Words that represent your specialities, Magic is playing dice with the universe.

To cast a spell, say what you want to accomplish with it, think of what can happen if it goes wrong, then wager something in return for the power you need.

  • If the spell goes wrong, you don’t lose whatever it is you wagered.

  • If it goes right, roll 1d6.

    • If the result is a number from 1 to 4, you lose the thing being wagered.

    • On a 5 or 6, you can keep what you gambled, but it’s somehow wrong now, as part of its own being has been spent to power your spell.

You can bet anything, from magical artefacts to your own soul, though souls are cheaper than they sound.

The higher your bet, the higher your rewards. The weirder or more emotionally significant your wager is to you, the more complex or powerful the desired effect.

If you bet one of your tags, which means betting a piece of yourself, or something with similar importance to your character (e.g., going back to square one on a want, sacrificing a loved one), you always succeed. Roll a d6 just to see how big your success was:

  • On a 1 or 2, your success was barely mediocre,

  • On a 3 or 4, it was just alright,

  • And on a 5 or 6, it was awesome.

  • This die also says if you lose the tag (if your result was from 1 to 4) or if you keep it but it’s all wrong now (on a 5 or 6). Tell everyone else how that happens.

If you bet an artefact, or something of similar weird or supernatural properties, or if you take a condition, you can cast the spell the same way as with Magic Words. That means you roll a d6 for each thing you bet and take the highest result. Each die also determines if you lose the stuff you wagered or not.

For every other thing being gambled, decide among the table how valuable it is for your character, on a scale from 1 to 5. Roll a d6 under or equal that number to cast the spell. If the spell goes wrong, you don’t lose whatever it is you wagered. If it goes right, roll the d6 to see if you keep it (but changed) or lose it.

Artefacts

Artefacts are magical items with very specific usages. They can also be sacrificed to fuel spells.

You can start with one magical artefact in addition to your HAVE tags. You can acquire more during your journeys, up to 3 at once if you can use Magic, and 2 if you don’t.

An artefact can have either a passive or active effect.

Whenever you activate an active-effect artefact, roll a d6. A result of 5 or 6 means it’s depleted.

Passive artefacts change you over time. Every consecutive day of using them, roll a d6. A 5 or 6 means it has a random unexpected side-effect, which you can decide.

Example artefacts

1. A translation amulet (passive) that allows the wearer to understand any language spoken, but not to speak it.

2. A cursed blade (active) that when coated with the user’s blood can cut through any material.

3. The mummified tongue of a monk (active) that can transport you to any place you have in mind when licking it.

4. A hair band (active) that allows you to rapidly grow your hair up to 10 meters while wearing it.

5. A tarot deck (active) that depicts 56 common items and 22 stranger versions of ordinary objects. Whenever you shuffle and draw a card, you can burn it to make the item materialise.

6. A piece of paper (active) that looks like any official ID you need. You have no control over what it says, but it will represent authority for the beholder.

7. A set of two mirrors (passive) that show each other’s reflections.

8. An ugly yellow beanie (passive) that makes its wearer look extraordinarily beautiful to everyone.

9. A giant eyeball (active) that allows you to see through the eyes of a given person, provided you feed it some bodily fluid from the target.

10. A pair of sneakers (passive) that allow you to walk over any surface, at any angle.

11. A flashlight (active) that allows you to see otherworldly creatures, spirits, demons and other immaterial beings.

12. A matching set of jewellery (passive) that allows the wearers to communicate silently with each other.

The Bureau

The Bureau are the People in Black Suits and Dark Shades. You know the ones, and they know you know. Careful, they may be watching you right now.

The Bureau can also be known as The Agency, The Foundation, Los Hermanos and other vague epithets like that. Their actual name probably forms a 3-letter acronym. They are the ones who seek to preserve ‘normalcy’ at all costs: containing, attacking and sometimes exterminating everything they see as a threat to them. They operate in secrecy, but their reach and influence are vast.

Note that The Bureau is not inherently evil. Sometimes their job becomes necessary: some supernatural or anomalous objects, phenomena and beings do represent a threat to others, human or not, and may need to be dealt with in some way. The Bureau is not your ally, but that doesn’t mean it will always be on the opposite side as you.

If you decide to use The Bureau in your game, think about what your characters currently know about it. Are they on its radar, and if so, do they know? Have they only heard rumours and whispers about it, or are they in an open conflict already? And what is The Bureau anyway? Is it an independent organisation? A secret branch of a government? Maybe an international task force under the UN? Just the CIA? Is it restricted to secret agent types, or does it have military or scientific assets at its disposal? How big is its operation? You might treat them as cops, as a vast conspiracy, as civil servants that truly believe they protect the world against the forces of darkness, as all of the above. Just remember to keep them as shadowy as you can and that curiosity killed the cat.

Encountering the Bureau

  1. You arrive at a familiar place (your home? your workplace? a usual haunt?) and find two people wearing black suits and dark sunglasses, questioning everyone. They seem off. Do you confront them?
  2. You find a small camera hidden inside your house. As soon as you pick it up, you hear an engine starting. You run to the window just in time to see a white van leaving. It had been parked at the end of the street all week.
  3. You manage to break into one of their facilities. What were you looking for? No matter what it was, what you find are a dozen brightly lit cells. Most of them are occupied by dirty, emaciated anomalous beings.
  4. You turn a corner towards a magical place you know and stumble on a raid going on. A huge armoured vehicle is parked nearby. There are agents everywhere. They are ready to breach the entrance. One of them sees you and asks you to go away for your own safety.
  5. There have been rumours of monster attacks around the city lately. Multiple sources can confirm they’re true, but none can provide more details. They ask for your help. You better figure this out before the Bureau gets involved
  6. While investigating the actions of one of the other factions below, you encounter an agent of the Bureau. Reluctantly, they ask you for a temporary alliance. Do you trust them?

The Watchers of the Many-Angled One

Neonazi occultists, guided by a resurrected Julius Evola. Usually, they would be just your typical nazi punchable annoyance, but now they’re toying with dangerous forces that they don’t even comprehend. The group is composed of a network of independent cells, coordinated by an inner circle called the Lodge. Only a few people in the Lodge know about Evola, who now goes by the name Father Enoch, the Watchers’ Master Immortal.

The Watchers worship a Something they call the Many-Angled One, which supposedly inhabits a dimension simultaneously above and below our own, represented as a shifting mass of straight lines crossing at impossible angles. Some call it Abhoth, others Choronzon, but it is forbidden for the Watchers to say or even know its True Name until one is deemed worthy. You may guess what those guys think is worthy.

As expected, they plan to summon the Many-Angled One to our reality, to cleanse the Earth and give birth to a new Aeon of triumph with their god’s guidance – ‘triumph’ here meaning White Men ruling not just our world, but the whole solar system. In the meantime, they prepare for Its coming by committing as many atrocities as they can. They believe extreme acts of violence can awaken their innate capacity to see the world from the nonhuman, polyangular perspective of their Lord by transcending what they see as the human weaknesses in them. Oh, and while they’re at it they also use their little magical knowledge to try and make the world more to their liking, associating with paramilitary and terrorist groups, funding Far-Right parties and pundits, hoarding magical relics, and trying to spread their beliefs both esoteric and political as much as they can. Some of them become cops and military just so they can kill without consequences and offer those deaths as blood sacrifices to their god. Some get into politics, some into finance. There are probably sleeper Watchers inside all the other factions detailed here.

If you want to use the Watchers in your game, don’t worry about making them complex antagonists; don’t worry about making them have a point; don’t worry about making them charismatic. They don’t need any of that. They’re nazis that want to summon a cosmic horror. Just punch them and be done with it.

The Brotherhood of Thoth

They’re very, very rich, therefore they’re complete assholes. Hoarders that call themselves collectors. Robbers that call themselves scholars. Some are in it for knowledge, others for power, others for fun. Many are just there because they can.

They gather in sumptuous places like mansions and country estates, surrounded by pictures of dead people and priceless artefacts. They show each other their new acquisitions, their weird theories, and their future plans. They feast on the flesh of sentient magical creatures, cooked with the most expensive ingredients.

The Brotherhood is like every typical occultist society that popped up across the last couple of centuries. A bunch of rich guys (yes, they’re usually guys) with too much time on their hands. It’s about ownership, in the end. They desire to learn the secrets of the world just to reassert their power over it.

If you want to use the Brotherhood in your game, think about how rich people are bastards how they would see your characters. If you’re human, do they see you as inferior because of class or as peers in knowledge? If not, do they find you amusing, dangerous, maybe useful? Or maybe tasty?
